Don't Spoil Your Supper! Keeping Food Safe During a Power Outage

2026-07-03 22:58 UTC · 💡 How-To

A sudden power outage can throw a wrench into your day, halting everything from your morning coffee to your evening meal. While losing power is an inconvenience, it also poses a serious risk to the food in your refrigerator and freezer. With recent reports of outages, like the one impacting DTE customers, knowing how to protect your food from spoilage and prevent foodborne illness is more important than ever.

Before the Lights Go Out: Preparation is Key

The best defense against food spoilage during an outage is preparation. A few simple steps can make a big difference:

  • Stock Up on Essentials: Keep a supply of non-perishable foods that don’t require cooking or refrigeration, like canned goods, peanut butter, and crackers.
  • Get a Thermometer: Place an appliance thermometer in both your refrigerator and freezer. Your fridge should be at or below 40°F (4°C), and your freezer at 0°F (-18°C) or below.
  • Freeze Water or Gel Packs: Fill plastic bottles with water and freeze them to create additional cold sources. These can help keep food cold longer in your fridge or a cooler.
  • Plan for Ice: Have plenty of ice on hand or know where you can get some quickly.

During the Blackout: Keep it Cold

Once the power goes out, your main goal is to keep perishable foods as cold as possible for as long as possible:

  • Keep Doors Closed: Resist the urge to frequently open your refrigerator and freezer doors. An unopened refrigerator can keep food safe for about 4 hours, while a full, unopened freezer can maintain safe temperatures for approximately 48 hours (24 hours if half-full).
  • Group Food Together: If you have space, group foods together in your fridge and freezer. This helps them stay colder as a mass.
  • Use Ice or Frozen Packs: Move refrigerated food to a cooler packed with ice or frozen water bottles if the outage is prolonged beyond 4 hours.

After the Power Returns: When to Discard

Once the power is back on, it’s time to assess your food. Remember: when in doubt, throw it out!

  • The 4-Hour Rule: Any perishable food (meat, poultry, fish, eggs, dairy, and leftovers) that has been above 40°F (4°C) for more than 4 hours should be discarded.
  • Check Temperatures: Use a food thermometer to check the temperature of food items. If they're above 40°F, they're not safe.
  • Don't Taste Test: Never taste food to determine its safety. If it looks or smells suspicious, or has been in the danger zone too long, it's best to err on the side of caution.
  • Freezer Assessment: Food in the freezer is safe if it still contains ice crystals or is as cold as if it were in the freezer. You can refreeze it.

Being prepared and knowing these crucial steps can save you money, prevent food waste, and most importantly, keep you and your family safe from foodborne illness during an unexpected power outage. Stay safe, and keep that food fresh!
