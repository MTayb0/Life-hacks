Will We Ever Get More Biscuits? The Buzz Around Ted Lasso Season 4

2026-07-12 01:49 UTC · 🔥 Trending

Few shows have captured hearts quite like Ted Lasso. With its charmingly optimistic coach, a cast of lovable misfits, and a seemingly endless supply of witty banter and heartwarming moments, the Apple TV+ sensation became a cultural touchstone. But after a third season that felt, to many, like a fond farewell, a burning question remains on everyone's minds: will there be a Season 4?

The Finale That Felt Like a Farewell

Season 3 brought many character arcs to satisfying conclusions. Ted Lasso himself finally returned to Kansas to be with his son, while Richmond AFC, against all odds, achieved promotion and a strong finish in the Premier League. Many supporting characters found their footing, whether in love, career, or self-discovery. It was a finale that offered closure, tying up loose ends with neat, sentimental bows, leading most viewers to believe it was the intended end of the series.

Hope Springs Eternal: The Cast and Creators Weigh In

Despite the conclusive feel, the show's creators and cast have been careful not to slam the door shut entirely. Star and co-creator Jason Sudeikis has often spoken about the three-season arc he envisioned for Ted's journey, but he's also hinted at the possibility of continuing stories within the 'Ted Lasso universe,' perhaps focusing on other characters or in a different format. Brendan Hunt (Coach Beard) and Brett Goldstein (Roy Kent), also writers on the show, have echoed this sentiment, suggesting that while Ted's story might be complete, the world of AFC Richmond could live on. It's less a definitive 'no' and more a tantalizing 'not yet, and maybe not in the way you expect.'

Why Fans Are Still Asking for More

In a world often filled with cynicism, Ted Lasso offered a much-needed dose of earnest optimism, kindness, and humor. Its messages about empathy, growth, and the power of believing in yourself resonated deeply with audiences. Fans aren't just attached to the plot; they're attached to the feeling the show evokes. The idea of no more Monday morning biscuit deliveries, no more 'Believe' signs, and no more Ted-isms is a tough pill to swallow. Whether it’s hope for a spin-off focusing on Keeley’s PR firm, a full-fledged Season 4 with a new premise, or even a feature film, the yearning for more Richmond magic remains strong.

So, while the future of Ted Lasso in its original form hangs in delightful ambiguity, the ongoing speculation around Season 4 proves just how much this charming show means to its dedicated fanbase. For now, we wait, we believe, and we hope for just a few more biscuits.
