The Bitter Battle for Miraval: Pitt vs. Jolie Continues

2026-07-03 19:02 UTC · 📰 News

In the world of celebrity divorces, few have captured public attention quite like that of Brad Pitt and Angelina Jolie. While their marriage ended years ago, a complex and increasingly bitter legal battle over their once-shared French winery, Château Miraval, continues to unfold, offering a rare glimpse into the high-stakes complexities of disentangling a multi-million-dollar empire.

Château Miraval, a picturesque estate in Correns, France, isn't just a vineyard; it's a symbol of their life together. It was where the couple married in 2014, a family home, and, crucially, the source of a highly successful rosé wine business. With its idyllic setting and critically acclaimed products, Miraval was more than an investment; it was part of their personal history.

The current legal drama centers on Jolie's 2021 sale of her 50% stake in Miraval to Tenute del Mondo, a subsidiary of Stoli Group, a company controlled by Russian oligarch Yuri Shefler. Pitt quickly sued, alleging that Jolie sold her share without his consent and in direct violation of a prior agreement that neither party would sell their interest without giving the other the right of first refusal. Pitt’s legal team argues that Jolie sought to inflict harm upon him by selling to a "stranger" with "poisonous associations and intentions."

Jolie, through her legal representatives, has countered these claims, asserting that she had every right to sell her share and that Pitt’s lawsuit is an attempt to maintain control and retaliate against her. Her team has previously stated that she sold her stake to regain financial independence and to distance herself from a business associated with their troubled past. This past includes allegations of abuse made by Jolie against Pitt, which Pitt has denied.

The legal wrangling has been extensive, involving courts in both the U.S. and Luxembourg, where the holding company for Miraval is based. Documents filed by Pitt’s lawyers describe Miraval as his "passion," arguing that he built the winery into the global success it is today. Meanwhile, Jolie’s filings suggest she couldn't continue to be involved in a business associated with the allegations she has made. Each side seeks to invalidate the other's claims, with billions potentially at stake beyond just the winery's value, impacting future business partnerships and reputations.

As the Miraval saga continues to unfold in court, it serves as a fascinating, if sometimes troubling, case study. It highlights how even after a divorce is finalized, the complexities of shared assets, personal histories, and business empires can fuel prolonged and public legal battles. For now, the future of Château Miraval, and the wine it produces, remains entangled in the celebrity spotlight.
